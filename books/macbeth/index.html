Text, sources and early records

Before the modern stage record, Macbeth has a print history, a source history and an early performance witness.

1611 / 1623

Forman’s note and the First Folio

  • Simon Forman’s account of seeing Macbeth at the Globe is the earliest surviving eyewitness record normally cited for the play in performance.
  • Macbeth was not printed in quarto.
  • The 1623 First Folio is the first printed text of the play.
  • The Folio text is unusually short among Shakespeare’s tragedies.

1587 / c.1606

Holinshed, Banquo and James I

  • Shakespeare drew on Holinshed’s Chronicles, especially the account of King Duncan, Macbeth and Banquo.
  • Holinshed presents Banquo as part of the Scottish history Shakespeare reshaped for the stage.
  • The play’s attention to kingship, succession and witchcraft belongs to the Jacobean court culture in which James VI of Scotland became James I of England in 1603.
  • James had published Daemonologie in 1597, before he came to the English throne.

Restoration and eighteenth-century stage history

The play’s stage life after the reopening of the theatres was shaped by adaptation, music, spectacle and star actors.

1664-1668

Davenant’s Restoration Macbeth and Pepys

  • Sir William Davenant’s adaptation was performed by the Duke’s Company after the Restoration.
  • The version added more music, songs, dance and spectacle for the witches.
  • Samuel Pepys saw Macbeth repeatedly between 1664 and 1668.
  • On 5 November 1664 Pepys recorded that the play was “admirably acted”.
  • Davenant’s version remained influential on stage well into the eighteenth century.

1744 / 1747

Garrick and Pritchard

  • David Garrick revived Macbeth in 1744.
  • Garrick advertised the play as Shakespeare’s text but still retained Restoration stage business associated with Davenant.
  • He cut material from Shakespeare’s play and wrote a new death speech for Macbeth.
  • Hannah Pritchard first played Lady Macbeth opposite Garrick in 1747.
  • Garrick later removed the play from his repertoire after Pritchard retired.

1785

Sarah Siddons as Lady Macbeth

  • Siddons first played Lady Macbeth at the Theatre Royal, Drury Lane, on 2 February 1785.
  • William Beechey painted Siddons with the emblems of tragedy in 1793.
  • Her Lady Macbeth became one of the defining stage associations of the role.

Nineteenth-century theatre, opera and visual culture

Macbeth became part of actor-manager theatre, transatlantic rivalry, opera and Victorian image-making.

1820-1851 / 1849

Macready, Forrest and the Astor Place Riot

  • William Charles Macready played Macbeth across more than thirty years, beginning at Covent Garden in 1820.
  • Macready’s farewell performance was Macbeth at Drury Lane on 26 February 1851.
  • On 10 May 1849, rival Macbeth performances by Macready and Edwin Forrest were part of the background to the Astor Place Riot in New York.
  • Macready performed at the Astor Opera House; Forrest performed nearby at the Broadway Theatre.
  • More than twenty people were killed.

1847 / 1865

Verdi’s opera Macbeth

  • Verdi’s Macbeth was first performed at the Teatro della Pergola, Florence, on 14 March 1847.
  • The opera used a libretto by Francesco Maria Piave, with later contribution from Andrea Maffei.
  • Verdi revised the opera for Paris in 1865.
  • The revised version added material required by French grand-opera practice, including ballet music.

1888 / 1889

Henry Irving and Ellen Terry, Lyceum Theatre

  • Henry Irving’s Macbeth opened at the Lyceum Theatre, London, on 29 December 1888.
  • Irving played Macbeth opposite Ellen Terry as Lady Macbeth.
  • Arthur Sullivan composed incidental music for the production.
  • Sargent saw Terry on opening night and painted her as Lady Macbeth in 1889.
  • Terry’s green dress was decorated with iridescent beetle wings; the National Trust holds the costume at Smallhythe Place.

Twentieth-century changes

Modern dress, public theatre, Black performance history and studio staging changed the look and scale of Macbeth.

1923 / 1936

Barry Jackson; Orson Welles and the Federal Theatre Project

  • Barry Jackson’s 1923 Birmingham Repertory Theatre production is often cited as a major early modern-dress Macbeth.
  • Orson Welles directed Macbeth for the Federal Theatre Project’s Negro Theatre Unit.
  • The Welles production opened at the Lafayette Theatre, Harlem, on 14 April 1936.
  • The setting was moved from Scotland to a Caribbean island associated with Haiti.
  • Jack Carter played Macbeth and Edna Thomas played Lady Macbeth.

1976-1978

Trevor Nunn: Ian McKellen and Judi Dench

  • The production opened at The Other Place on 9 September 1976.
  • It transferred to Newcastle, the Royal Shakespeare Theatre, the Donmar Warehouse and the Young Vic.
  • Thames Television filmed the production between 7 and 12 March 1978.
  • Ian McKellen won the 1976 Plays and Players London Theatre Critics’ Best Actor award.
  • Judi Dench won the 1977 SWET Best Actress in a Revival award.

1987 / 1999 / 2011 / 2018 / 2023

Later company records

  • Yukio Ninagawa’s Macbeth played at the National Theatre’s Lyttelton Theatre; the 1987 Olivier Awards listed Ninagawa and designer Kappa Senoh for work including Macbeth.
  • Gregory Doran’s 1999 RSC production starred Antony Sher and Harriet Walter.
  • Michael Boyd’s 2011 RSC production starred Jonathan Slinger and Aislín McGuckin.
  • Polly Findlay’s 2018 RSC production starred Christopher Eccleston and Niamh Cusack.
  • Wils Wilson’s 2023 RSC production starred Reuben Joseph and Valene Kane.

Recent stage productions and afterlives

A small selection of modern records with clear dates, venues, cast and production facts.

2007 / 2010

Rupert Goold: Patrick Stewart and Kate Fleetwood

  • Rupert Goold’s production opened at Chichester Festival Theatre in 2007.
  • Patrick Stewart played Macbeth and Kate Fleetwood played Lady Macbeth.
  • The production transferred to the West End and then Broadway.
  • The television version was broadcast on BBC Four on 12 December 2010 and in the United States on PBS Great Performances.
  • The filmed version was shot at Welbeck Abbey.

2021

Yaël Farber: James McArdle and Saoirse Ronan, Almeida Theatre

  • The Almeida production ran from 2 October to 27 November 2021.
  • Yaël Farber directed.
  • James McArdle played Macbeth.
  • Saoirse Ronan made her UK stage debut as Lady Macbeth.
  • The Almeida page lists production photographs by Marc Brenner.

2023-2025

Max Webster: David Tennant and Cush Jumbo, Donmar Warehouse

  • The Donmar production opened after previews from 8 December 2023 and ran until 10 February 2024.
  • Max Webster directed.
  • David Tennant played Macbeth and Cush Jumbo played Lady Macbeth.
  • The production used binaural sound, with audience members wearing headphones.
  • The filmed stage production was scheduled for cinema release from February 2025.

2010 / 2013 / 2023

Other modern records

  • David Greig’s Dunsinane, a sequel to Macbeth, was staged by the RSC in 2010 and later toured by the National Theatre of Scotland.
  • Kenneth Branagh and Rob Ashford co-directed Macbeth for Manchester International Festival in 2013; Branagh played Macbeth opposite Alex Kingston as Lady Macbeth.
  • Simon Godwin directed a site-specific Macbeth starring Ralph Fiennes and Indira Varma in 2023-24.

Screen productions

Film entries are listed as production records: director, cast, release and major production facts.

1948 / 1957 / 1971 / 2015 / 2021

Major screen versions

  • Orson Welles directed and starred in Macbeth in 1948 for Republic Pictures / Mercury Productions.
  • Akira Kurosawa’s Throne of Blood relocated the play to feudal Japan and was released in 1957.
  • Roman Polanski’s 1971 film starred Jon Finch and Francesca Annis, with a screenplay by Polanski and Kenneth Tynan.
  • Justin Kurzel’s 2015 film starred Michael Fassbender and Marion Cotillard.
  • Joel Coen’s The Tragedy of Macbeth starred Denzel Washington and Frances McDormand and was released by A24 and Apple TV+.
