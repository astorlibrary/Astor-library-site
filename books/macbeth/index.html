Macbeth: productions, images, records.

Production records, casts, dates, venues, archive images and source links. No plot summary. No generic interpretation.

Archive focus. This page collects performance records, credited images and public source links for teachers, students and readers using the Astor Library edition.

Earliest records, Restoration changes and eighteenth-century acting

Entries here are limited to verifiable performance records, printed records, named productions and archive images.

1611 / 1623

Forman’s record; First Folio text

  • Simon Forman’s notes are usually cited as the first surviving eyewitness record of a performance of Macbeth at the Globe.
  • The play was first printed in the 1623 First Folio as The Tragedie of Macbeth.
  • The Folio text is short compared with Shakespeare’s other tragedies, which is why production and textual history often overlap in discussions of the play.

1664-1668

Davenant’s Restoration Macbeth and Pepys’s diary

  • Sir William Davenant’s adaptation was performed by the Duke’s Company after the Restoration.
  • The version added more music, songs, dance and spectacle for the witches.
  • Samuel Pepys saw Macbeth repeatedly between 1664 and 1668.
  • On 5 November 1664 Pepys recorded that the play was “admirably acted”.
  • Davenant’s version remained influential on stage well into the eighteenth century.

1744 / 1747

David Garrick and Hannah Pritchard

  • David Garrick revived Macbeth in 1744.
  • Garrick advertised the play as Shakespeare’s text, but still retained Restoration stage business associated with Davenant.
  • He cut material from Shakespeare’s play and wrote a new death speech for Macbeth.
  • Hannah Pritchard first played Lady Macbeth opposite Garrick in 1747.
  • Garrick later removed the play from his repertoire after Pritchard retired.

1785

Sarah Siddons as Lady Macbeth

  • Siddons first played Lady Macbeth at the Theatre Royal, Drury Lane, on 2 February 1785.
  • William Beechey’s 1793 portrait presents Siddons with a tragic mask and dagger.
  • Later portraits and memorials helped fix Siddons as Britain’s great tragic actress.

Nineteenth-century theatre and opera

Macbeth moved through star rivalry, actor-manager theatre, opera, spectacle and Victorian visual culture.

1820-1851 / 1849

Macready, Forrest and the Astor Place Riot

  • William Charles Macready played Macbeth across more than thirty years, beginning at Covent Garden in 1820.
  • Macready’s farewell performance was Macbeth at Drury Lane on 26 February 1851.
  • On 10 May 1849, rival Macbeth performances by Macready and Edwin Forrest were part of the background to the Astor Place Riot in New York.
  • Macready performed at the Astor Opera House; Forrest performed nearby at the Broadway Theatre.
  • Contemporary accounts put the deaths at more than twenty, with many more injured.

1847 / 1865

Verdi’s opera Macbeth

  • Verdi’s Macbeth was first performed at the Teatro della Pergola, Florence, on 14 March 1847.
  • The opera used a libretto by Francesco Maria Piave, with later contribution from Andrea Maffei.
  • Verdi revised the opera for Paris in 1865.
  • The Paris version added material required for French grand-opera practice, including ballet music.
  • The revised version became the usual basis for many modern performances.

1888 / 1889

Henry Irving and Ellen Terry, Lyceum Theatre

  • Henry Irving’s Macbeth opened at the Lyceum Theatre, London, on 29 December 1888.
  • Irving played Macbeth opposite Ellen Terry as Lady Macbeth.
  • Arthur Sullivan composed incidental music for the production.
  • Sargent saw Terry on opening night and painted her as Lady Macbeth in 1889.
  • Terry’s green dress was decorated with iridescent beetle wings; the National Trust holds the costume at Smallhythe Place.

Twentieth-century breaks

Modern dress, Federal Theatre, studio staging and screen versions changed what Macbeth could look like.

1923 / 1936

Barry Jackson; Orson Welles and the Federal Theatre Project

  • Barry Jackson’s 1923 Birmingham Repertory Theatre production is often cited as the first major twentieth-century modern-dress Macbeth.
  • Orson Welles directed Macbeth for the Federal Theatre Project’s Negro Theatre Unit.
  • The Welles production opened at the Lafayette Theatre, Harlem, on 14 April 1936.
  • The setting was moved from Scotland to a Caribbean island associated with Haiti.
  • The cast was Black; Jack Carter played Macbeth and Edna Thomas played Lady Macbeth.
  • The production became widely known as the “Voodoo Macbeth”.

RSC production record

The Royal Shakespeare Company’s Macbeth archive lists major pairings from 1952 onward and gives detailed pages for selected productions.

1976-1978

Trevor Nunn: Ian McKellen and Judi Dench

  • The production opened at The Other Place on 9 September 1976.
  • It transferred to Newcastle, the Royal Shakespeare Theatre, the Donmar Warehouse and then the Young Vic.
  • Thames Television filmed the production between 7 and 12 March 1978.
  • Ian McKellen won the 1976 Plays and Players London Theatre Critics’ Best Actor award.
  • Judi Dench won the 1977 SWET Best Actress in a Revival award.

1999 / 2001

Gregory Doran: Antony Sher and Harriet Walter

  • Gregory Doran’s modern-dress production was staged at the Swan Theatre.
  • Antony Sher played Macbeth and Harriet Walter played Lady Macbeth.
  • The RSC archive records a 2001 film version connected to this production.
  • RSC image captions identify combat gear for the Weird Sisters and a production design built around light, darkness and the Swan stage.

2011

Michael Boyd: Jonathan Slinger and Aislín McGuckin

  • Michael Boyd’s RSC production featured Jonathan Slinger as Macbeth and Aislín McGuckin as Lady Macbeth.
  • RSC records identify the venue as the Royal Shakespeare Theatre.
  • Production photographs show three cellists above the stage and repeated use of supernatural children.
  • The set, designed by Tom Piper, included fractured stained glass and damaged religious imagery.

2018

Polly Findlay: Christopher Eccleston and Niamh Cusack

  • Polly Findlay directed the 2018 RSC production.
  • Christopher Eccleston played Macbeth and Niamh Cusack played Lady Macbeth.
  • The production played in Stratford-upon-Avon and later at the Barbican, London.
  • RSC production photographs credit Richard Davenport and list Michael Hodgson as the Porter, Edward Bennett as Macduff and Luke Newberry as Malcolm.

2023 / 2025

Recent RSC entries

  • The RSC’s past productions page lists Wils Wilson’s 2023 production with Reuben Joseph and Valene Kane.
  • Recent press listings identify a 2025 RSC production directed by Daniel Raggett at The Other Place, with Sam Heughan as Macbeth and Lia Williams as Lady Macbeth.
  • The 2025 production is useful for comparison with the 1976 Nunn production because both are tied to The Other Place.

Major twenty-first-century stage productions

Recent records are added when they have a theatre page, press images, cast record or archive page.

2007 / 2008 / 2010

Rupert Goold: Patrick Stewart and Kate Fleetwood

  • Rupert Goold’s production opened at Chichester Festival Theatre in 2007.
  • Patrick Stewart played Macbeth and Kate Fleetwood played Lady Macbeth.
  • The production transferred to the West End and then to Broadway.
  • The television version was broadcast on BBC Four on 12 December 2010 and aired in the United States on PBS Great Performances.
  • The filmed version was shot on location at Welbeck Abbey.

2021

Yaël Farber: James McArdle and Saoirse Ronan, Almeida Theatre

  • The Almeida Theatre production ran from 2 October to 27 November 2021.
  • Yaël Farber directed.
  • James McArdle played Macbeth.
  • Saoirse Ronan made her UK stage debut as Lady Macbeth.
  • The Almeida page lists eight production photographs by Marc Brenner.
  • Creative team credits include Soutra Gilmour for set, Joanna Scotcher for costume, Tim Lutkin for light and Peter Rice for sound.

2023-2025

Max Webster: David Tennant and Cush Jumbo, Donmar Warehouse

  • The Donmar Warehouse production opened after previews from 8 December 2023 and ran until 10 February 2024.
  • Max Webster directed.
  • David Tennant played Macbeth and Cush Jumbo played Lady Macbeth.
  • Theatre Weekly’s first-look record lists Rosanna Vize as designer, Bruno Poet as lighting designer, Gareth Fry as sound designer and Shelley Maxwell as movement director.
  • The production used binaural sound, with audience members wearing headphones.
  • Live music came from an onstage Scottish folk band led by Alasdair Macrae, with Kathleen MacInnes.
  • The filmed stage production was scheduled for cinema release from February 2025.

Screen productions

Film entries are listed as production records: director, cast, release and major production facts.

1948

Orson Welles, Republic Pictures

  • Directed and produced by Orson Welles.
  • Starring Welles as Macbeth and Jeanette Nolan as Lady Macbeth.
  • Music by Jacques Ibert; cinematography by John L. Russell.
  • Released at the Venice Film Festival in 1948.
  • The film was made for Republic Pictures through Mercury Productions.

1957

Akira Kurosawa, Throne of Blood

  • Directed, co-written, produced and edited by Akira Kurosawa.
  • Starring Toshiro Mifune and Isuzu Yamada.
  • The film relocates Macbeth from Scotland to feudal Japan.
  • Released theatrically in Japan on 15 January 1957 by Toho.
  • It was the first film screened at the first BFI London Film Festival on 16 October 1957.

1971

Roman Polanski and Kenneth Tynan

  • Directed by Roman Polanski.
  • Screenplay by Polanski and Kenneth Tynan.
  • Starring Jon Finch as Macbeth and Francesca Annis as Lady Macbeth.
  • Music by the Third Ear Band; cinematography by Gil Taylor.
  • The production companies included Playboy Productions and Caliban Films.

2015

Justin Kurzel: Michael Fassbender and Marion Cotillard

  • Directed by Justin Kurzel.
  • Written for screen by Todd Louiso, Jacob Koskoff and Michael Lesslie.
  • Starring Michael Fassbender as Macbeth and Marion Cotillard as Lady Macbeth.
  • Production companies included StudioCanal, Film4, DMC Film, Creative Scotland and See-Saw Films.
  • The film was selected for the 2015 Cannes Film Festival official selection.

2021

Joel Coen: Denzel Washington and Frances McDormand

  • Written and directed by Joel Coen.
  • Starring Denzel Washington as Macbeth and Frances McDormand as Lady Macbeth.
  • Shot in black and white.
  • Released by A24 and Apple TV+.
  • The film received Academy Award nominations for Denzel Washington, cinematography and production design.
