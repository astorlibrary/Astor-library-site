Modern stage and broadcast records

Major records with named directors, cast and venue evidence.

2013 / 2014

Kenneth Branagh and Rob Ashford, Manchester International Festival / Park Avenue Armory

  • Rob Ashford and Kenneth Branagh co-directed Macbeth for Manchester International Festival 2013.
  • The production was staged in the deconsecrated Church of St Peter, Ancoats.
  • Branagh played Macbeth opposite Alex Kingston as Lady Macbeth.
  • Ray Fearon played Macduff.
  • The final performance was broadcast to cinemas on 20 July 2013 through National Theatre Live.
  • The production transferred to New York’s Park Avenue Armory in June 2014.
  • The Armory staging used a dirt stage, rain effects, battle action and mud as part of the production environment.

2018

Rufus Norris, National Theatre: Rory Kinnear and Anne-Marie Duff

  • Rufus Norris directed Macbeth at the National Theatre in 2018.
  • Rory Kinnear played Macbeth.
  • Anne-Marie Duff played Lady Macbeth.
  • The production played in the Olivier Theatre.
  • It was Norris’s first Shakespeare production in twenty-five years.

2023 / 2024

Simon Godwin: Ralph Fiennes and Indira Varma

  • Simon Godwin directed a site-specific Macbeth starring Ralph Fiennes as Macbeth and Indira Varma as Lady Macbeth.
  • The UK run included warehouse-style venues in Liverpool, Edinburgh and London.
  • The Washington engagement was presented by Shakespeare Theatre Company in a former television soundstage.
  • Emily Burns adapted the text.
  • Frankie Bradshaw designed the set and costumes.
  • The production was later available through National Theatre at Home.
  • Varma won a Helen Hayes Award for her performance.

Afterlives and adjacent Macbeth works

These are not straight Macbeth productions. They sit beside the production history because they extend Macbeth’s stage life.

2010 / 2011 onward

David Greig, Dunsinane

  • Dunsinane is David Greig’s sequel to Macbeth.
  • The play was staged by the Royal Shakespeare Company in 2010.
  • It includes Shakespeare’s Macduff and Malcolm while adding new characters including Siward’s English subordinate Lord Egham.
  • A BBC Radio 3 adaptation premiered on 30 January 2011.
  • The National Theatre of Scotland toured the RSC production from 2011.

1987

Yukio Ninagawa at the National Theatre

  • Yukio Ninagawa’s Macbeth played at the National Theatre’s Lyttelton Theatre.
  • The 1987 Laurence Olivier Awards listed Ninagawa in the Director of the Year category for Macbeth at the National Theatre Lyttelton and Medea at the National Theatre Olivier.
  • Kappa Senoh was listed in the Designer of the Year category for Macbeth at the National Theatre Lyttelton.
