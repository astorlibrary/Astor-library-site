Othello, William Shakespeare

Othello, first recorded in performance at Whitehall in 1604, is Shakespeare's tragedy of a Venetian general whose marriage is destroyed by deception. Othello has married Desdemona, the daughter of Brabantio, and is sent to Cyprus in the service of Venice. Iago, passed over for promotion, uses Cassio, Roderigo and the handkerchief to turn Othello against his wife. The play follows the movement from public honour to private accusation, ending in Desdemona's murder and Othello's recognition of what he has done.

This Astor Edition presents the play with a clear reading text and supporting material shaped for study. The edition draws on the 1622 Quarto and the 1623 First Folio, with notes on major textual choices where they affect the play. It also includes scene introductions, explanatory notes, source material on Cinthio, and contextual sections on early publication, performance history and later adaptations.

Records worth knowing

Short factual records for reading, teaching and cross-reference.

  • Whitehall record The Revels accounts record The Moor of Venis at court on 1 November 1604.
  • Q1 title page The 1622 quarto says the play had been acted at the Globe and Blackfriars by the King's Men.
  • Two early texts The 1622 Quarto and 1623 Folio differ in wording, stage directions, oaths and substantial passages.
  • Cinthio source Shakespeare takes the skeleton of the plot from Cinthio, but names Desdemona, develops Iago and creates a far more complex tragic structure.
  • Cyprus setting The Turkish threat is removed early; the remainder of the play focuses on internal collapse rather than battlefield action.
  • Object as evidence The handkerchief is not proof in itself. Iago makes it function as proof by controlling when and how it is seen.

    • Text and early publication

    Othello is one of the plays where early print history matters directly. The Quarto and Folio are close enough to compare, but different enough to change stage and reading effects.

    1622

    First Quarto

    • Printed by Nicholas Okes for Thomas Walkley.
    • The title page records performance at the Globe and Blackfriars by the King's Men.
    • It is the first printed version of the play, nearly twenty years after the first recorded performance.
    • Quarto stage directions are often fuller than those in the Folio.

    1623

    First Folio

    • The First Folio printed the play one year after the quarto.
    • Folio includes about 160 lines not found in the quarto.
    • These include well-known material such as the Willow Song and Othello's Pontic Sea speech.
    • The Folio omits many oaths and profanities that appear in the quarto, a difference often connected to post-1606 theatrical regulation.

    Editing

    Why textual choice matters

    • Modern editions often combine Quarto and Folio readings.
    • Choices can affect Desdemona's voice, Othello's rhetoric, Iago's language and staging detail.
    • A study edition should make major differences visible where they alter interpretation.

    Source history

    Shakespeare's source is Cinthio, but the play transforms a prose tale of adultery accusation into a tragedy of language, evidence and social vulnerability.

    Cinthio

    Hecatommithi

    • The plot comes from Giraldi Cinthio's Italian story collection.
    • Cinthio provides the Moor, the Ensign, the wife, the captain and the handkerchief plot.
    • Shakespeare names Desdemona and develops Iago's theatrical control of audience, victim and accomplice.
    • The source lacks the same intense opening in Venice and the same tragic self-recognition at the close.

    Venice and Cyprus

    State service and frontier pressure

    • Venice supplies a political world of senators, reputation, military office and public judgement.
    • Cyprus is a strategic possession exposed to Ottoman pressure.
    • After the Turkish fleet is destroyed by storm, the play turns from external war to internal corrosion.

    Knolles and the Turks

    Recent historical reading

    • Shakespeare probably drew some Turkish-war detail from Richard Knolles's General History of the Turks, published in 1603.
    • This helps explain why the play's Mediterranean politics feel current as well as classical or Italianate.

    Race and language

    Early modern terms

    • The play's language of Moor, blackness, service and otherness must be read historically and theatrically.
    • Othello is indispensable to Venice and still vulnerable to racialised suspicion.
    • Iago's plot works because social prejudice supplies him with available material.

    Context and argument

    The play moves from public authority to private control. Othello's tragedy depends on the collapse of judgement, not on simple jealousy alone.

    Marriage

    Desdemona's choice

    • Desdemona chooses Othello before the play begins.
    • Her marriage is defended publicly before the Senate.
    • Brabantio's accusation turns her desire into a legal and racial crisis.
    • Her speech in Act 1 matters because it establishes her agency before Iago's story starts to erase it.

    Iago

    Motives and improvisation

    • Iago names several motives: promotion, suspicion, resentment and appetite for control.
    • None of them fully contains him.
    • His power comes from improvising on what others already fear.
    • He makes ordinary social gestures appear criminal.

    Cassio

    Reputation and office

    • Cassio's demotion gives Iago the working structure for the plot.
    • His concern for reputation is not vanity only; military office depends on public trust.
    • Iago converts Cassio's courtesy to Desdemona into apparent sexual evidence.

    Emilia

    Witness too late

    • Emilia handles the handkerchief, but does not understand its future use.
    • Her final testimony breaks Iago's control of the story.
    • Her death marks the cost of truth arriving after the murder it might have prevented.

    Characters and functions

    Each major role is tied to a form of public or private interpretation.

    Othello

    General, husband, interpreter

    • Othello begins as a respected military servant of Venice.
    • His public eloquence is controlled, formal and persuasive.
    • In Cyprus, Iago teaches him to read signs through suspicion.
    • His final speech tries to shape the record of his own crime.

    Desdemona

    Choice, loyalty, vulnerability

    • Desdemona chooses marriage across family and social expectation.
    • She remains consistent in her support for Cassio because she believes in his case.
    • That consistency is reinterpreted by Iago as sexual persistence.
    • The Willow Song gives her a memory language that the plot has otherwise denied her.

    Iago

    Director of false evidence

    • Iago controls timing, overhearing, interruption and staged sight.
    • He speaks differently to each person.
    • His silence at the end refuses explanation just when the audience expects confession.

    Emilia

    Practical witness

    • Emilia sees marriage without romance and service without illusion.
    • Her speech about husbands and wives gives the play its strongest counter-argument to male sexual double standards.
    • Her final testimony restores the facts but cannot restore Desdemona.

    Production history and image records

    Production history is also a history of race, casting, military setting, blackface practice, modern-dress staging and the changing treatment of Iago.

    1604

    Whitehall and the King's Men

    • The first recorded performance was at Whitehall on 1 November 1604.
    • The 1622 quarto title page later records performances at the Globe and Blackfriars.
    • The play therefore has a court record and a public-theatre print record.

    1660

    Margaret Hughes and Desdemona

    • Restoration records associate Othello with the first appearance of a professional actress on the English public stage.
    • Margaret Hughes is traditionally identified with Desdemona in 1660.
    • The record matters because Shakespeare's female roles had previously been played by boys and young men on the public stage.

    nineteenth century

    Kean, Booth, Salvini and Irving

    • Major nineteenth-century Othellos include Edmund Kean, Edwin Booth and Tommaso Salvini.
    • Henry Irving played Iago opposite Edwin Booth's Othello in London in 1881.
    • The period helped define the play as a star tragedy of vocal scale, physical presence and racialised stage convention.

    2015

    Iqbal Khan at the RSC

    • Iqbal Khan directed the 2015 RSC production.
    • Hugh Quarshie played Othello and Lucian Msamati played Iago.
    • Msamati was the first Black actor to play Iago at the RSC.
    • The production made race, military hierarchy and institutional trust central to the staging.

    2022

    Clint Dyer at the National Theatre

    • Clint Dyer directed Othello at the National Theatre, with Giles Terera as Othello and Paul Hilton as Iago.
    • The production was noted as the first National Theatre production of the play directed by a Black director.
    • It placed the play's racial politics and public violence at the centre of the staging.

    Screen versions

    Screen adaptations usually choose between theatrical concentration and cinematic realism. Close-up changes the play because Iago's asides and Othello's visual misreadings can become private camera events.

    1952

    Orson Welles

    • Welles directed and starred in a film version released in 1952.
    • The film is known for its fragmented production history and strong visual style.
    • It turns the play into a dark, architectural and highly edited tragedy.

    1965

    Laurence Olivier

    • Stuart Burge directed the 1965 film based on the National Theatre production.
    • Laurence Olivier played Othello, with Maggie Smith as Desdemona and Frank Finlay as Iago.
    • The film preserves a major stage performance while also preserving the blackface practice now central to critical discussion of the play's performance history.

    1995

    Oliver Parker

    • Oliver Parker directed the 1995 film, with Laurence Fishburne as Othello and Kenneth Branagh as Iago.
    • Fishburne was the first Black actor to play Othello in a major English-language cinema film of the play.
    • The film foregrounds intimacy, sexuality and Iago's direct address to camera.

    2001

    O

    • Tim Blake Nelson's O relocates the plot to an American high-school basketball setting.
    • Mekhi Phifer plays Odin James, the Othello figure.
    • The adaptation shows how portable the structure of trust, envy, manipulated proof and social spectacle became.
