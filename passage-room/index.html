The Passage Room

Stay with the words.

A short passage can hold the design of a whole book. Here the original words stay open while the notes sit at the edge of the page. Read first. Then look again.

Astor method · 01

Begin with what is there: a repeated word, a turn in the sentence, an image that will not quite settle.

Astor method · 02

Use context where it changes the reading. Do not let it replace the reading.

Astor method · 03

Leave room for a difficulty that cannot be tidied away.

These are not lists of devices and they do not translate literature into a single answer. Each reading follows a few exact choices on the page and asks what those choices make possible.

You can use one before opening the full book, return to it after reading the chapter or scene, or take its final question into a lesson and argue with it.

Four close readings

Open at a sentence.

The first shelf moves from a soliloquy to a confession, an apology and a storyteller’s first judgment.

01Hamlet · Act 3, Scene 1
“To be, or not to be, that is the question”

A speech that sounds private, yet never once says “I”.

The room will grow

One page at a time.

More passages will be added across novels, plays, poetry and non-fiction. The aim is not to collect famous quotations. It is to build a place where difficult, beautiful writing can be read slowly and in company.
