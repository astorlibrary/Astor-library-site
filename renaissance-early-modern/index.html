Renaissance & Early Modern.

These works ask what a society should be and how power really works. They were written amid religious conflict, court politics, travel and the rapid growth of print.

Niccolò Machiavelli

The Prince

Machiavelli wrote this short treatise in 1513, drawing on Florentine politics, Medici rule and the example of Cesare Borgia; it was published in 1532. Arms, law, virtù, fortuna, fear and appearances all become part of one urgent question: how is power won and kept?

Thomas More

Utopia

More turns an imaginary voyage to a commonwealth into a searching humanist satire. Its debates about property, labour, religion and law never settle into a simple model: Utopia exposes the failures of European society while raising difficult questions of its own.
