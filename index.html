Why Astor?

A library name with a history

The name is a nod to one of the great library names of the nineteenth century. The original Astor Library in New York was founded through the bequest of John Jacob Astor. In 1895 it was combined with the Lenox Library and the Tilden Trust to form The New York Public Library.

It was not a circulating library. Its books were there to be consulted and studied. Astor Editions take that reference-library idea into the text itself, placing useful material around the original work.
