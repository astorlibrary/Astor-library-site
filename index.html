The idea behind the name

A reference library inside the book.

The original Astor Library in New York was a place where books were consulted and studied. Astor Editions take that idea into the text itself: the complete work remains at the centre, with the useful material placed around it.

Read straight through, or stop for a summary, a factual note or a piece of context. The help is there without taking the book away from you.

How the editions work

Help where it belongs.

In prose editions, each chapter has a clear summary and a factual context section. Shakespeare editions preserve the plays with scene divisions, line numbers and supporting material for classroom use.

The added material is there to help with understanding, context and analysis. It should never crowd out the reason for opening the book in the first place.
