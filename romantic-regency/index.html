Romantic & Regency.

Courtship and inheritance shape private lives, while new ideas about authorship, science and society unsettle the world beyond the drawing room.

1811

Sense and Sensibility, Jane Austen

Austen’s first published novel appeared anonymously in 1811. Elinor and Marianne respond very differently to love and disappointment, but both must reckon with inheritance, women’s financial dependence, marriage, secrecy and the discipline of feeling. Screen versions keep returning to that contrast.

1813

Pride and Prejudice, Jane Austen

First drafted as First Impressions and published in 1813, Austen’s novel tests first judgements against the hard facts of marriage, money and entail. Elizabeth Bennet, Darcy and Wickham are also a way into its free indirect style, key passages, GCSE study and rich screen history.

1818 / 1831

Frankenstein; or, The Modern Prometheus, Mary Shelley

Two Astor editions place the anonymous 1818 publication beside Shelley’s revised 1831 text. Reading them together reveals how Romantic science, galvanism and layered narration shape the Creature’s story, and why the novel has proved so adaptable on stage and screen.
