Two worlds, neither simple

Rome and Egypt organise the play, but Shakespeare repeatedly unsettles the judgements attached to each place.

Rome and Egypt

The opposition keeps changing

Roman characters call Egypt excessive and undisciplined, yet Rome is also theatrical, competitive and ruthless. The contrast tells us as much about the speaker as it does about either place.

Source and stage

History becomes performance

Shakespeare draws heavily on North’s Plutarch but gives public history an unstable theatrical life. Messengers, reports and ceremonies compete to determine what the audience believes.

The final acts

Defeat does not settle meaning

Caesar wins the political contest, but Cleopatra contests his control of the story. Her last performance asks whether imperial victory can ever fully command memory, image or desire.

A useful way in. When a character describes Rome, Egypt, Antony or Cleopatra, treat the description as an argument. The play rarely allows one voice to provide its final judgement.
