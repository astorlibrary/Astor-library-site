Using the free library

More than a worksheet.

Each resource begins with the text. A plot guide keeps cause and consequence clear; a passage guide slows down over language and form; a context page explains the history that the writing actually uses. The aim is to give a reader something substantial enough to return to the book with a better question.

The guides can be read on the site before the PDF is opened. That makes it easier to see exactly what a resource covers and to follow links to the relevant book page. Teachers may use them for planning or discussion; students can use them to revise a section, check an argument or begin closer reading. See every guide in the site index.

Astor resources are free.
Support helps keep them open for students, teachers and independent readers.
