Poetry.

A poem rewards the second look. These guides slow down a title, a turn, an image or a difficult piece of syntax, then put the detail back into the movement of the whole poem.

Every guide is free. Read the introduction on the site, then open the complete illustrated PDF. Use one to prepare a lesson, revise a set poem or sharpen an independent reading.

A way to use the guides

Read for movement, not labels.

Begin with what changes. A voice may turn towards an audience, an image may acquire a second meaning or the rhythm may resist what the sentence appears to say. That movement usually gives you more than a list of techniques.

Then return to the whole poem. A good quotation is not a loose piece of evidence. Ask where it appears, what has prepared it and what the next line makes us reconsider. The PDFs provide the detail; the judgement remains yours.
