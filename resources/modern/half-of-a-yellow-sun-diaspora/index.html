Inside the PDF

What this presentation is doing.

It is not a plot summary. The fourteen slides build a particular argument about exile, divided belonging and the authority to record a war.

The idea

Diaspora has a double edge

The presentation begins with a disagreement inside postcolonial thought. Exile can create distance from the stories a nation tells about itself, but displacement can also be imposed, traumatic and disabling. The useful tension is between seeing differently and losing the place from which a life once made sense.

Keep that tension open when reading the characters. The guide is strongest when ‘diaspora’ names a problem rather than becoming a label attached to anyone who crosses a border.

The history

Nation is treated as something made

A compressed sequence moves from colonial borders and British rule to the 1966 massacres, Biafran secession and the civil war. Its purpose is to show why home, citizenship and national loyalty cannot be separated from political history in the novel.

This is an argumentative account, not a neutral timeline. Where a slide gives a casualty figure, political motive or judgement about a leader, compare it with historical scholarship before presenting the claim as settled fact.

The novel

Three witnesses cannot produce one complete view

Richard, Olanna and Ugwu occupy different relations to Biafra. Richard chooses allegiance but remains marked as British; Olanna witnesses loss through privilege and intimate grief; Ugwu moves from domestic education into military complicity and the work of writing.

Reading the three together prevents ‘the narrator’ from becoming a single authority. Their limits, silences and unequal responsibilities are part of the novel’s design.

The form carries the argument.

Adichie does not place private life beside history as decoration. The broken order of the narrative changes how cause, responsibility and memory are understood.

  • Inverted chronologyParts Two and Three interrupt chronological order. The return to the earlier period places betrayal and fracture before the full account of war, making private division echo without simply explaining national conflict.
  • Limited knowledgeNo central character sees the whole war. News, rumour, direct witness and later understanding remain unevenly distributed, which makes certainty itself an object of scrutiny.
  • The missing sisterKainene’s disappearance denies the comfort of a confirmed fate. The absence remains active for Olanna and for the reader because narrative closure cannot restore what history has taken.
  • The unwritten bookRichard wants to write Biafra, but the final authorship belongs elsewhere. The question is not only what happened; it is who has earned the right to shape the record and whose experience an outsider may claim.
  • Ugwu’s responsibilityHis movement towards authorship does not erase his participation in sexual violence. Testimony and moral damage occupy the same life, preventing the witness from standing safely outside the history he records.
  • Domestic spaceHouses, kitchens, classrooms and refugee rooms register political change at human scale. Loss of home is material before it becomes a theory of exile.

    • How to use the argument.

    A critical lens becomes useful when it helps you notice exact choices in the novel and remains open to challenge.

    Begin in a scene

    Let the novel set the terms

    Choose a moment of departure, return, divided loyalty or failed narration. Establish who sees it, which details the focal character notices and what the narrative withholds. Only then bring in the larger language of diaspora or nationalism.

    Keep levels separate

    Character, form and history are not interchangeable

    A character’s opinion is not automatically the novel’s final position. A structural pattern is not proof of one historical cause. State clearly whether you are analysing a voice, the arrangement of the narrative or the political record beyond the fiction.

    Test the lens

    Ask what the theory cannot explain

    Does the language of diaspora illuminate every displaced character equally? What changes when exile is chosen, enforced or internal to a nation? The strongest essay uses the presentation as a proposition to examine, not a conclusion to repeat.
