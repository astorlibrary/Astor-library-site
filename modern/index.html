Here twentieth-century political anxiety turns strange: one novel becomes a nightmare chase, the other a farmyard revolution.

1908

The Man Who Was Thursday, G. K. Chesterton

First published in 1908, Chesterton’s novel follows Gabriel Syme from Saffron Park into undercover policing, anarchist disguise and the Council of Days. What begins like a detective story becomes a nightmare allegory shaped by Edwardian politics and the elusive Sunday, with a later life on radio and screen.

1945

Animal Farm, George Orwell

Orwell turns the history of the Russian Revolution and Stalinism into a short, unsettling allegory. Around Boxer and Squealer, propaganda and rewritten commandments slowly alter life on the farm. The story was later retold on stage and screen. Sales availability varies by country because copyright terms differ.
