Printed in quarto in 1600 and in the First Folio in 1623, the comedy sets two courtships against one another in Messina. Beatrice and Benedick’s wit meets Hero and Claudio’s crisis of overhearing, false evidence and public shame, with Dogberry and later screen versions completing the story.

The Gray’s Inn performance record and First Folio text place this early comedy beside its Plautine sources. In Ephesus, two sets of twins turn debt, marriage and supposed magic into mistaken identity before recognition restores order; musical afterlives extend the joke.
