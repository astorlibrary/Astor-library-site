Begin with epic from the ancient Mediterranean: stories of war, exile and homecoming whose meanings have shifted through translation and retelling.

Virgil

The Aeneid

Virgil’s Roman epic begins in the aftermath of Troy and follows Aeneas towards a new home in Italy. Dryden’s 1697 translation carries the story through Dido, the underworld and the war with Turnus. Its account of exile and empire belongs to Augustan Rome, but its afterlife has been much longer.
